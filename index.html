The independent advantage

Why use an independent broker over a big bank?

Walk into a branch and you are talking to a salesperson with one product shelf. They cannot tell you a competitor has a sharper rate, because they are not allowed to know and not paid to care. Their job is to keep you inside the building.

An independent broker inverts that. We hold accreditations across 40+ banks, mutuals and non-bank lenders, and the Best Interests Duty, a legal standard banks are not held to, requires us to recommend the loan that serves you. Not the one that serves a quarterly target.

The difference compounds. The right structure on day one, an offset used properly, a rate repriced every year instead of never. Over a 30-year loan that is not a rounding error. It is years of your life back.

This is why brokers now write the clear majority of new Australian home loans. Borrowers worked out where the leverage is. It is not at the branch counter.

The quiet cost of staying put

What is the bank loyalty tax?

Banks advertise their sharpest rates to win new customers, then quietly leave existing customers on higher back-book rates. The gap between the two is the loyalty tax, and Reserve Bank research has repeatedly confirmed it: the longer you stay, the more you pay for the identical product.

The bank is betting you will never check. Most people lose that bet for a decade.

Straight answers

The questions people actually ask

How do mortgage brokers get paid, and what does it cost me?

The lender you settle with pays us a commission. You pay nothing. And because the Best Interests Duty makes it illegal to recommend a loan because of its commission, the recommendation engine only points one way: at your interest. The rate you get through us is the same or better than walking in directly, because we negotiate from the wholesale side of the counter.

Will using a broker slow down my approval?

Usually the opposite. We see every lender's live turnaround times and credit appetite, so your file goes to a lender that wants your scenario this week, packaged exactly how their assessors expect it. A branch lodges into one queue and hopes.

I already have a loan. Is it worth checking?

If you have not repriced in the last 18 months, almost certainly. The loyalty tax means your rate has probably drifted above what your own bank offers new customers today. A reprice request from a broker often gets a cut without even refinancing. If they will not move, forty other lenders will.

What makes a boutique broker better than a big franchise broker?

Scale is the bank's advantage, not yours. A boutique desk means the person who built your strategy is the person who answers your call in year six. No handovers, no junior pools, no "your broker has left the business." Your 30-year plan deserves a 30-year relationship.

Is the 40+ lender panel just marketing? Do you really use them all?

We genuinely hold the accreditations, and the long tail matters. The sharpest deal for a self-employed borrower, a low-deposit first home buyer or a six-property investor is almost never at a major bank. It is at a mutual or non-bank you have never heard of, and finding it is the entire job.
